Concept

先看见一只标准小笼包，再去寻找它的来处。

The experience begins with a small tasting at Fuchun Xiaolong, where guests can see the finished dumpling: thin skin, fine pleats, hot broth, and the local way to eat it. Then we walk into Guangyuan Wet Market to decode the ingredients behind that bite before cooking with a Shanghainese auntie in a private home-style kitchen.

4-hour main experience

主体验行程

Citywalk is kept separate as an optional neighborhood suggestion before or after the experience. The hosted program focuses on xiaolongbao, market life, and the private kitchen.

14:30

富春小笼馆集合与观察

Meet at Fuchun Xiaolong for a light tasting. Guests observe the shape, pleats, broth, and learn the local way to eat xiaolongbao: make a small opening first, sip the hot soup carefully, then enjoy it with vinegar and ginger.

15:00

广元菜场采购

Walk to Guangyuan Wet Market to buy pork, scallion, ginger, flour-related ingredients, and seasonal market finds while learning how locals shop.

15:45

前往私人家庭式厨房

Move to a nearby home-style kitchen opposite the market, hosted by a local Shanghainese family.

16:00

上海阿姨小笼包教学

Learn filling, wrapping, pleating, and steaming. Guests make their own dumplings with hands-on guidance.

17:30

品尝与家庭餐桌交流

Taste the freshly steamed xiaolongbao and talk about Shanghainese home cooking around a private family table.

18:30

体验结束

The experience ends on site. Private transfer is not included.

Market textures

菜场不是背景，是这只小笼包的第一间厨房。

The market visit is designed around the logic of the dumpling: meat for texture, aromatics for fragrance, flour for skin, and local stalls for the rhythm of everyday Shanghai.

Nearby map guide

体验前后，可以顺路感受这一带的上海。

These optional stops are close to Guangyuan Road and work well before or after the hosted xiaolongbao experience. They are not part of the 4-hour guided program, but they help guests understand the neighborhood around the market and home kitchen.
以下为体验前后可自行安排的周边玩法参考，不计入 4 小时带队体验。

1.1 km | 30-45 min

武康路 Wukang Road

Less than 1.2 km long, Wukang Road feels like a compact index of Shanghai's modern history: former residences of writers, diplomats, artists, and political figures sit among plane trees and old villas in many architectural styles. It is one of the easiest places nearby to feel haipai culture through a slow citywalk.

1.2 km | 30-45 min

华山绿地 & 幸福路 Huashan Green Space

A romantic pocket of greenery near Xingfu Road. The park now has a pink Xi Bridge, a small chapel-like structure that arches over water and brings a playful sense of ceremony. Nearby Xingfu Road and the marriage registration office add a sweet meaning: happiness, vows, and a softer side of Shanghai.

1 km | 45-60 min

徐家汇 Xujiahui

A nearby commercial hub with malls, cafes, and an elevated pedestrian bridge that is especially good for evening city lights. Guests can use it as an easy post-experience option for shopping or a short night-view walk; in daytime, Xujiahui Park is a calmer green alternative.

Practical notes

天气、排队与确认事项

Shanghai in mid-June can be warm and rainy. The experience keeps walking short and uses indoor spaces as the anchor.

If Fuchun is crowded, we keep the tasting light, use takeaway if needed, and move quickly into the market section.
If it rains or feels too hot, optional neighborhood walking is reduced. The main experience remains Fuchun, market, kitchen, and tasting.
Before final confirmation, please verify the Fuchun branch, Guangyuan market afternoon ingredient condition, and the auntie/kitchen availability for June 13.

Hosted by Lu

把上海真正生活着的一面，带给第一次来到中国的客人。

Lu has lived in Shanghai for 10 years. With a background in brand marketing and years of vlogging, she knows the city's main streets, side lanes, markets, riversides, and everyday food scenes.

Many travelers' first idea of Chinese food comes from Chinese restaurants in their own hometowns. Once they arrive in China, Lu hopes to offer something more local and specific: the way people actually shop, cook, eat, and talk around a table.

China has many famous landmarks and viral photo spots, but Lu is more interested in connecting visitors with local life. Her experiences usually begin with food, then open into family kitchens, neighborhood markets, and the small cultural details that make a city feel personal.

常驻上海 10 年，Lu 熟悉上海的大街小巷，也擅长把本地美食和文化特色转译给海外游客。相比只去网红景点，她更希望让客人进入真实的本地生活：逛菜场、听故事、一起动手做饭，再从一顿饭里理解一座城市。
